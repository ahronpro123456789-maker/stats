Objective 3: One-Way ANOVA — Differences in ProQOL by Demographics

Parametric analysis of variance with assumption tests and post-hoc comparisons

Objective 3 examines whether there are significant differences in compassion fatigue (secondary traumatic stress and burnout) and compassion satisfaction levels when grouped by age, gender, and professional role. The One-Way Analysis of Variance (ANOVA) was employed as the parametric test. Assumption checks include Levene's test for homogeneity of variances and the Shapiro-Wilk test for normality. When the ANOVA yields a significant result, post-hoc pairwise comparisons with Bonferroni correction are conducted to identify which specific groups differ. Effect size is reported as eta-squared (η²). The significance level is set at α = 0.05.

💡 Overall Conclusion

Across 9 One-Way ANOVA tests examining the three ProQOL subscales by three demographic variables, 0 test(s) yielded statistically significant results and 9 were not significant at α = .05. No significant differences were found for any combination of demographic variable and ProQOL subscale. This suggests that age, gender, and professional role do not significantly influence the levels of compassion satisfaction, burnout, or secondary traumatic stress among the DV responders in this sample. Overall, respondents exhibited moderate compassion satisfaction (M = 39.42), moderate burnout (M = 24.63), and moderate secondary traumatic stress (M = 26.0), indicating that while DV responders maintain professional fulfillment, attention to compassion fatigue prevention remains important.
